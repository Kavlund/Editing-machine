Setup · One-time

Connect your Editing Machine

Follow the steps below. As each connection goes live, its light turns green automatically. This page checks every few seconds. Nothing here can affect your videos. It only wires up delivery.

You only do steps 1 and 2 (giving your editor access). Your editor sets up 3, 4 and 5 for you while you watch each light turn green. That's it.
Google Drive Notion Slack
1

Give your editor access (GitHub)

You do this
  1. Open your repository on github.com.
  2. Go to Settings → Collaborators → Add people.
  3. Add savas-web and set the role to Admin.
2

Give your editor access (Railway)

You do this
  1. Open your project on railway.app.
  2. Go to Settings → Members → Invite.
  3. Invite your editor's email as a Member.
3

Google Drive: where finished videos land

Your editor Not connected
  1. In Google Cloud, create a service account and download its JSON key. Enable the Google Drive API.
  2. In Google Drive, create one root folder. Share it with the service account's email as Editor. Copy the folder ID from the URL.
  3. In Railway → Variables, add: GOOGLE_SERVICE_ACCOUNT_JSON, GDRIVE_ROOT_FOLDER_ID, GDRIVE_SHARE_ANYONE=true.

When it connects, adding a client auto-creates their <Client>/Edited/ folder.

4

Notion: link videos to your CPS cards (optional)

Your editor Not connected
  1. Create a Notion internal integration and copy its token.
  2. Share your CPS database with that integration.
  3. In Railway → Variables, add: NOTION_API_KEY, NOTION_CPS_DATABASE_ID, NOTION_DELIVERY_MODE=drive_link.
5

Slack: get pinged when a cut is ready (optional)

Your editor Not connected
  1. At api.slack.com/apps: Create New App → From scratch, pick your workspace.
  2. Open Incoming Webhooks → On → Add New Webhook to Workspace, choose a channel, Allow.
  3. Copy the webhook URL. In Railway → Variables, add SLACK_WEBHOOK_URL.

You'll get: "✅ Jordan Blake — 'Morning Routine' is done. Go have a look." with the link.
